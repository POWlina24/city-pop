City Pop:
Urban pop music for those with urban lifestyles

City Pop is a form of Japanese pop music that emerged in the late 1970s and peaked in the 1980s, heavily inspired by the western music of the time. It includes a wide range of styles – including AOR, soft rock, R&B, funk, and boogie – with Hawaiian and Okinawan tropical flourishes. It is associated with Japan’s leisure class and the country’s economic boom. It had largely been neglected by Westerners and seen by many Japanese as cheesy, until it's' revival in 2018.

City Pop was influenced by the tech that came out during that time like the walkman or cars with built-in cassette decks and FM stereos and various electronic musical instruments such as the Casio CZ-101 and Yamaha CS-80 synthesizers. In some tracks, you can even hear the tech in them like a cassette being inserted to play at a start of a song to a full fake radio narration in a compilation album like in Tatsuro Yamashita’s Come along 1 and 2.
